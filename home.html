Single Cell Explorer

Single Cell Explorer is designed to establish a collaborative platform between experimental biologists and bioinformatics scientists. High dimensional data contains tremendous amounts of information, and directly exploring the data can be more useful than just reading the companion article.

Goals

  1. Establish a collaborative platform between experimental biologists and bioinformatics scientists
  2. Enable experimental biologists to explore single cell sequencing datasets to understand both the heterogeneity of cells and samples and technical challenges. This will facilitate better study design with larger sample sizes
  3. Allow experimental and computational scientists to collaborate on refining parameters and algorithms.
  4. Make single cell data available for everyone.

Collaborative Cell Type Identification

Choosing single cell cluster identification parameters can be ambiguous. The following plot shows various clustering results using different resolution parameters by the leiden algorhism (scanpy.api.tl.leiden function). It is not straightforward to determine which resolution to use. A resolution of 1, the default value, produces too many clusters in comparison with ground true.

The ground truth can be reflected by the following UMAP, which was painted by known PBMC marker genes. This process can be done by biologists or domain experts (Immunologists) using Single Cell Explorer, by leveraging prior knowledge. The result actually indicates a resolution of 0.2 might best reflect the current knowledge of PBMC cell type classification.

Without programming skills, biologists can study interesting cell clusters based on t-SNE or UMAP using freehand selection of cell clusters, followed by checking cell marker expression or identifying unknown cell markers using Single Cell Explorer. Such coordination between Computational Biologists and Experimental Scientists will make analysis and decisions much faster. Unannotated cells can be left to colleagues or other experts to examine. The results can be shared after t-SNE or UMAP have been generated. Annotation can be used for further analysis.
